## What Is a Kickoff Meeting?

A **kickoff meeting** is the **first formal meeting** where the project team and stakeholders come together to:

- Establish a **shared vision**    
- Align on **project goals and scope**    
- Clarify **individual roles and responsibilities**    
## Who Attends?

Invite the following participants:
- **Project team members** (as identified in the RACI chart)    
- **Project sponsor**    
- **Key stakeholders**    
### RACI Chart Reminder:

- **Responsible**: Who does the work?    
- **Accountable**: Who makes decisions?    
- **Consulted**: Who provides input?    
- **Informed**: Who needs updates?    

---
## Why a Kickoff Meeting Is Important

- Ensures **everyone is aligned** on goals and responsibilities.    
- Builds **team rapport** and encourages **collaboration**.    
- Provides a platform to **ask questions** and surface **concerns or insights**.    
- Sets expectations for **communication and execution**.    

---

## Planning and Running a Kickoff Meeting

### Suggested Agenda (1 Hour Total)

|Time|Agenda Item|Purpose|
|---|---|---|
|10 min|**Introductions**|Build rapport and clarify team roles|
|5 min|**Project Background**|Explain why the project exists and set the vision|
|5 min|**Goals and Scope**|Define what's in-scope vs. out-of-scope and share key milestones|
|5 min|**Roles and Responsibilities**|Clarify what each team member is responsible for|
|10 min|**Collaboration and Tools**|Discuss tools (e.g., Asana, shared docs) and communication norms|
|10 min|**Next Steps and Expectations**|Set short-term action items and explain what comes next|
|15 min|**Q&A and Feedback Session**|Field questions, encourage feedback, build trust|
### Collaboration Tips:

- Share tools like spreadsheets, work management platforms, or project dashboards.    
- Define communication methods (e.g., daily emails, chat tools, weekly stand-ups).    

---

## Inclusive Practices During the Meeting

- **Shared Document for Questions**: Encourage team members to add thoughts or feedback in writing.    
- **Confidence Poll**: Ask team members to rate their understanding (1–5 scale) and offer support if confidence is low.    
- **Follow Through**: Deliver on any promises or support requests to build trust and reliability as a PM.    

---
## Logistics and Best Practices

- **Send Agenda in Advance**: Share the finalized agenda 1–2 days before the meeting.    
- **Assign a Note-Taker**: Ask a teammate to document:    
    - Key discussion points        
    - Action items per team member        
- **Optional Recording**: Consider recording the session for later reference—obtain permission first.    
- **Follow-Up Email**:    
    - Summarize key outcomes and next steps        
    - List action items        
    - Invite any remaining questions or comments        

---
## Summary

The kickoff meeting helps unify the team and lay the groundwork for successful project execution.

**Meeting Agenda Should Cover:**
- Introductions    
- Project background and purpose    
- Goals and scope    
- Roles and responsibilities    
- Tools and collaboration plans    
- Upcoming tasks and expectations    
- Team questions and feedback
