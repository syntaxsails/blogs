## Step 1: Evaluate the Project as a Whole

- **Review your project charter** to refocus on the overall goal.    
- List **all major activities** required to meet the project objectives.    
- Identify **key checkpoints** that represent:    
    - The completion of a **deliverable**        
    - The transition between **project phases**        

> ✅ These become your **milestones**  
> 🛠️ Smaller steps (like internal tasks) become **project tasks**

### Example: Office Green – Project Plant Pals

|Deliverable: Launch a New Website|
|---|
|✅ **Milestones**:|
|- Approval of website design|
|- Completion of website development|
|- Implementation of user feedback|
|🛠️ **Tasks** (supporting milestones):|
|- Create initial design mockups|
|- Build a landing page|

---

## Step 2: Set Reasonable Deadlines for Each Milestone

### Consider:

- **Time required** to complete all tasks that lead up to each milestone    
- **Team input** on how long individual tasks will take    
- **Stakeholder expectations** for seeing progress    
### Tips for Setting Deadlines:

- Avoid scheduling multiple milestones **too close together** on large projects.    
- **Space them out** to allow teams time to complete supporting tasks.    
- Factor in:    
    - Review cycles        
    - Resource availability        
    - Dependencies between milestones        

---
## Collaborate with Your Team

- **Discuss timelines** with team members involved in each milestone.    
- Gather **time estimates** for each task leading to the milestone.    
- Use those estimates to create **realistic and achievable** deadlines.    

---
## Keep Stakeholders in Mind

- Understand **when stakeholders expect progress** to be shared.    
- Align milestone deadlines with **key stakeholder review dates**.    
- Regular milestones offer visibility into project health and reinforce accountability.    

---
## Summary: Setting Milestones

1. **Review the full project**    
2. Identify **key checkpoints** = milestones    
3. Break out **supporting tasks**    
4. **Estimate time requirements**    
5. Set **realistic deadlines**    
6. Consider **stakeholder needs** and expectations
