# Comprehensive Project Plan Components

A project plan serves as a living roadmap for your team. Beyond the core elements of tasks, milestones, people, documentation, and time, a comprehensive project plan integrates several other crucial components.

## Interconnected Project Plan Components

At the center of any project plan is the project schedule, which helps estimate project completion time and track progress against goals. In addition to the schedule, a comprehensive project plan should include:

### Project Scope and Goals

These are initially captured in your project charter, a document that defines key project details. Linking the project charter in your project plan provides easy access to objectives, reminding the team of their aims and preventing scope creep.

### Work Breakdown Structure (WBS)

A WBS is a hierarchical tool that organizes project milestones and tasks in the order they need to be completed. It breaks down work into manageable pieces. Your project plan should clearly display tasks with descriptions, owners, and due dates, enabling the team to understand responsibilities and deadlines. Detailed milestones and their statuses should also be included to visualize progress. Additionally, linking documentation like a RACI chart, which defines roles and responsibilities, is a best practice for transparency and effective communication.

### Budget

The project budget, heavily dependent on key project elements, should be linked to the project plan for smoother management and visibility. While you may not always be the primary budget manager, especially in larger organizations, regular check-ins with the responsible department are essential to stay informed about budget tracking.

### Management Plans

Integral to keeping a project organized and on track, management plans such as the change management plan, risk management plan, and communication plan should be linked within your project plan. These will be discussed in more detail in later lessons.

### Key Takeaway

Project scope and goals, the Work Breakdown Structure (WBS), the budget, and various management plans are all vital components that define how basic project plan elements (tasks, milestones, people, documentation, and time) are structured and utilized. Project plans are unique; knowing your end goals and what is essential to your team will help you assemble the relevant pieces.
