# The Role of Soft Skills

Time estimation, effort estimation, and capacity planning are all essential for creating an accurate project schedule. At the heart of this planning process is your team. Teammates are best positioned to provide accurate estimates for tasks and understand their own capacity. Engaging soft skills is crucial for obtaining the most accurate information from them.

**Soft skills** are personal characteristics that enable effective collaboration with others, encompassing communication and interpersonal abilities. They are vital for understanding potential obstacles to a teammate's performance.

Here are three ways to leverage soft skills for gathering accurate estimates from your teammates:

## 1. Asking the Right Questions

Approach conversations about time estimation as an interview, connecting with teammates to understand their work process for specific tasks. To gather relevant details, ask

**effective, open-ended questions** that cannot be answered with a simple "yes" or "no".

**Example (Office Green Project)**:

Instead of asking, "Can you complete the mock-ups in one week?" (a closed-ended question) , ask an open-ended question like, "How long does it typically take you to mock up a website design like this one?".

Follow-up questions can include:

- "How complex are the steps to complete this task?"    
- "What are the risks associated with this task?"    
- "When do you think you can have this ready?"    

Asking effective, open-ended questions helps you understand your teammates' work styles and tasks. Over time, this will enhance your understanding of their roles and tasks, reducing your reliance on them for every estimate.

## 2. Negotiating Effectively

As a project manager, your role involves bridging the gap between high-level project goals and the team's day-to-day perspective. Team members may have competing priorities from other projects. Effective negotiation can influence a team member to prioritize your project by finding a collaborative outcome that works for everyone.

**Example**: If a web designer estimates two weeks for mock-ups, but you hoped for one week, gently challenge the estimate with follow-up questions. You might ask if the estimate includes multiple pages and if a few pages could be shared sooner. This helps determine the estimate's flexibility or if additional resources are needed. Effective negotiation fosters shared ownership over project outcomes and helps create a schedule aligned with everyone's workload.

## 3. Practicing Empathy

**Empathy** is the ability to relate to the thoughts and feelings of others. Practicing empathy at work is an effective way to build trust within your team. Recognize that teammates are human and have limited capacity.

When discussing estimates, show empathy by asking about their overall workload, including work outside your project, and their work-life balance. Inquire about scheduled vacations, leave, or crucial holidays that might affect their availability. This proactive approach helps avoid assigning tasks when teammates are unable to complete them on time.

**Example**: If a web designer is also working on another website with an overlapping timeline, you might collaborate with the other project manager to balance workloads across teams, preventing designer overload. Showing appreciation for your team's work, collaboration, and support is also a vital part of empathy, as people value their contributions.

In summary, asking the right questions, negotiating effectively, and practicing empathy are key to obtaining viable and accurate project task estimates from your teammates. These estimates will then be put to good use in the project plan.
