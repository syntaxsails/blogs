While models may vary (e.g., newer or older than the **IBM z14**), most mainframes share a common structural layout and key components.

---
## Core Hardware Components

### 1. **CPC Drawers (Central Processor Complex)**

- Contains:    
    - **Processors**        
    - **Memory modules**        
    - **PCIe fanouts** – Interfaces for PCIe I/O cards        
    - **Flexible Support Processor (FSP)** – Provides redundant access to internal management networks        

> _Note: Called "drawers" because they slide out like drawers in a nightstand._

---
### 2. **DCA Inverters**

- Two inverters for **redundant power delivery**    
- Ensure stable and continuous power supply to system components    

---

### 3. **Cooling System**

- **Radiator pumps** circulate refrigerant to maintain optimal temperatures    
- Airflow:    
    - **Cold air** enters from the **bottom**        
    - **Hot air** exits from the **back**        
- Some systems use **water-cooling**, connecting to site-wide chilled water infrastructure    

---
### 4. **PCIe I/O Drawer**

- Houses all **input/output devices**, including:    
    - **Crypto cards**        
    - **Network adapters**        
    - **Compression cards**        
    - **Phi cards** (I/O-specific hardware)        

---

### 5. **Backup Batteries**

- Two large batteries    
- Provide **graceful shutdown** in the event of a power outage    
- Protect against **transaction loss** during power failures    

---
### 6. **Power Supplies**

- Up to **four power supplies** for redundancy    
- Often split across separate power panels to avoid single points of failure    

---

### 7. **Support Elements**

- Located at the top of the frame:    
    - **Display keyboard**        
    - **Two servers** known as **Support Elements**        
- Manage system operations and configuration    

---
## System Form Factors

### 1. **Single Frame Design**

- Known as **Business-Class Machines**    
- Compact form factor for environments with smaller hardware requirements    

### 2. **Rack-Mount Design**

- Fits into a standard **19-inch industry rack**    
- Allows mainframes to be co-located with other IT equipment (servers, storage, networking gear)    
- No longer needs to be isolated in a dedicated room    

---
## Summary

Mainframes like the IBM z14 are engineered with **redundancy, scalability, and efficient cooling** in mind. Whether deployed as a full frame, business-class model, or in a standard rack, they integrate seamlessly into modern IT environments while maintaining enterprise-grade reliability.
